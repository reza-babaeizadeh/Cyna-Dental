What is the Canadian Dental Care Plan?

The Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) is a federal government initiative designed to make dental care more affordable for Canadians who don't have access to private dental insurance. It's one of the most significant expansions of public dental care in Canadian history.

No Private Insurance Required

Designed specifically for Canadians without access to employer or private dental coverage.

Income-Based Coverage

Sliding scale benefits based on your family's adjusted net income.

Comprehensive Coverage

Covers preventive, basic, and major dental services including cleanings, fillings, and dentures.

CDCP Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the Canadian Dental Care Plan, you must meet all of these criteria:

Canadian Resident

Must be a Canadian resident for tax purposes with a valid Social Insurance Number

No Dental Insurance

Cannot have access to private or employer-sponsored dental insurance coverage

File Tax Returns

Must have filed your tax return for the previous year to verify income eligibility

Income Under $90,000

Family's adjusted net income must be less than $90,000 per year

CDCP Coverage Levels

Your coverage percentage depends on your family's adjusted net income

100%

Full Coverage

CDCP covers 100% of eligible dental services

Income Under $70,000

No out-of-pocket costs for covered services

60%

Partial Coverage

CDCP covers 60% of eligible dental services

Income $70,000 - $79,999

40% co-payment required

40%

Basic Coverage

CDCP covers 40% of eligible dental services

Income $80,000 - $89,999

60% co-payment required

How to Apply for CDCP

Simple steps to access your dental care benefits

1

Check Eligibility

Visit the official CDCP website or contact Service Canada to verify your eligibility status

2

Submit Application

Apply online through your My Service Canada Account or by calling 1-833-537-4342

3

Receive Benefits Card

Once approved, you'll receive your CDCP benefit card in the mail within 2-3 weeks
